What is carbon footprint? what is the need to know about it?

A carbon footprint is a measure of the total greenhouse gas emissions, primarily carbon dioxide (CO2) and other gases, associated with human activities such as energy use, transportation, and consumption. Understanding the concept of a carbon footprint is crucial for individuals, businesses, and governments to address climate change.

Why is it important? Why do we calculate it?

The carbon footprint is vital because it quantifies our individual and collective impact on climate change.This information is crucial for informed decision-making, policy development, and environmental accountability. By understanding and reducing our carbon footprints, we can take meaningful steps to mitigate global warming, limit the severity of climate change, and protect the planet's long-term health and sustainability.
